At first glance, it seemed nothing more than brittle parchment traced with fading ink—but the longer she stared, the more the lines shifted, as though alive. Paths appeared where none had been, winding toward the forbidden forest the villagers dared not name. Along its edges, tiny symbols pulsed like whispers caught in ink. Elin felt it then—a quiet pull, insistent and strange—calling her beyond the safety of home and into the secrets of the Whispering Woods.

Elara spread the map across her small wooden table, weighing its curling edges down with smooth river stones. The ink seemed to shimmer in the candlelight, revealing details she hadn’t noticed before—hidden trails, strange markings, and a symbol that pulsed faintly near the heart of the forest. She traced the path with her finger, noting how it twisted deeper into the Whispering Woods than any villager dared to go. As she watched, the map shifted ever so slightly, as if guiding her, urging her to follow.

A thrill of fear and excitement stirred within her. The stories she had heard—of voices in the trees and paths that led travelers astray—echoed in her mind, yet they only strengthened her resolve. Folding the map carefully, Elara packed a small satchel with bread, a flask of water, and her father’s old lantern. She paused at the threshold of her home, taking one steady breath before stepping out into the cool dawn. Without looking back, she set her course toward the Whispering Woods.
