Elara stepped into the Chronomancer’s inner sanctum, and the world outside seemed to vanish behind her like a closing door. The chamber was vast, circular, and impossibly still, its walls inscribed with rotating bands of glowing runes that shifted positions as if breathing. At the center stood an enormous clock with no hands, its face fractured into floating shards of light suspended in midair.

Each shard reflected a different moment—some she recognized, others she had never lived. The air trembled with a low, rhythmic pulse, like time itself was aware of her presence. Elara tightened her grip on the map, now almost fully faded, and took a hesitant step forward as the sanctum responded—slowly, deliberately—awakening.
